Tech Talk

DDD Melbourne

I once gave a talk at DDD Melbourne on why not everything needs machine learning which, yeah, is a bit odd coming from someone who works in ML. But at the time, a lot of people were trying to force it into every problem like it was the answer to everything.

Working in AI, I’ve seen a fair bit of hype. Machine learning gets thrown at things it doesn’t always belong. I think it’s worth being clear about where it fits, and where it doesn’t.

1st Place

Green Battery Hackathon

We built a trading system for the Australian energy spot market using real data from AEMO, goal was to maximize profit across simulated trades.

We tried everything from traditional strategies with Optuna tuning, to reinforcement learning, to custom ML setups, but what stuck was a neural network trained on a custom loss: max profit - actual profit.

For the pitch, we framed the idea from a business perspective — not just the tech, which helped us stand out. We ended up taking first place for the pitch, and second for the algorithm itself.
