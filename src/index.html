What I do

AI Systems for Data, Compliance, and Climate

I work at the intersection of machine learning, automation, and environmental intelligence. My focus is on building real-world systems that turn data into insight — whether for ESG reporting, geospatial analysis, or deep learning research.

  • I build end-to-end ML workflows — from experimentation to deployment — with automation in mind. Tools include PyTorch, FastAPI, HuggingFace, and vector databases. Ideal for productizing R&D and enabling AI agents.

  • I develop tools that process and analyze climate, satellite, and compliance data — from spatial pipelines to computer vision-based monitoring. Perfect for Scope 3 analysis and ESG insights.

  • My work often involves building machine learning systems grounded in physical laws, using physiinformed neural nets and modeling-heavy data. I speak data — from sensors to structure.

  • From high-altitude balloons to vision-based monitoring, I build prototypes that connect physical systems to AI backends. Bridging hardware, firmware, and CV logic.

How I Work

How I Work

I work best when blending technical precision with practical outcomes. Whether it's a geospatial model, an AI prototype, or a data pipeline for compliance — my approach is always grounded, iterative, and transparent.

  • 01

    Understand the Problem

    I dive into your real-world goals first — not just the data. That means working with domain experts, CFOs, or researchers to align on outcomes.

  • 02

    Design the Right System

    From automation workflows to deep learning pipelines, I choose tools and models that match the complexity, scale, and constraints of your use case.

  • 03

    Build & Iterate

    I build in short, testable iterations — with frequent check-ins, clear docs, and deployable code. Every solution is engineered to be maintainable and reproducible.

  • 04

    Deliver, Document, Support

    From launch to handover, I make sure systems are clearly explained and easy to adopt. I'm available for ongoing support or integration into your stack.
